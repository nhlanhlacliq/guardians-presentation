1. Executive Summary
This document presents the full solution architecture and delivery proposal for the Guardians of the Future 2026 Pilot Platform. It is written from the perspective of the solution architect responsible for translating the vision described in the Guardians Platform Brief into a buildable, testable, and maintainable system.
The Guardians platform is an action-tracking and community engagement system designed to make real-world environmental and social impact visible — at the level of the individual, the Circle (local team), the city, and the global network. The 2026 pilot is not the full vision. It is a focused, time-bounded experiment designed to answer six core learning questions about participation, reporting, and the recognition of impact.
The pilot scope is deliberately constrained. This proposal recommends building only what is necessary to answer those questions, with a clean technical foundation that can scale. The architectural premise is simple: one shared data model, the Impact Record, powers every user-facing feature. Progress views, Contribution Profiles, and Impact Summaries are all read operations on a single, well-structured table.
▌
Architectural principle: We are not building five separate tracking systems. We are building one clean data foundation with a set of well-designed views on top. Getting the Impact Record right is the single most important technical decision in the entire pilot.

3. Pilot Definition — Module by Module
The following sections define what the pilot must include for each must-have module, what is explicitly out of scope for the pilot, and the key decisions or risks associated with each.
3.1 Registration
Registration is the entry point to the platform. The pilot design prioritises speed and low friction above completeness.
In scope for pilot
Name and display name
Contact email (used for authentication)
Location: city and region (used for city-level progress aggregation)
Language preference (drives i18n column selection)
Magic link authentication - email-based, no password required
Explicitly out of scope
Complex onboarding flows or multi-step wizards
Social login (OAuth) — add post-pilot if needed
Profile photo upload at registration — can be added later
▌
Architect's note: Email-link authentication (magic link) eliminates password management, reduces friction, and is a mature, well-supported pattern in Supabase Auth. It is the right choice for the pilot.
3.2 Guardians Narrative Space
This module establishes the initiative's identity and orientates new participants. For the pilot, it is primarily a content module, not a dynamic feature.
In scope for pilot
Initiative story and purpose - clear, readable, not static 'About' dump
Orientation content for new Guardians (what this is, how to participate)
Curated stories or reflections - manually curated at launch
Explicitly out of scope
Dynamic feedback loop where Circle stories feed back into the narrative - post-pilot feature
User-generated narrative content submission flows
3.3 Circles - Simplified for Pilot
Circles are the organisational unit of the platform - local teams that adopt challenges and accumulate a shared impact history. For the pilot, a Circle is a page, a team, and a shared history. Nothing more.
In scope for pilot
Public-facing profile: name, location, short description, current lifecycle status, members count
Lifecycle state management: Forming → Active → Resting → Concluded (visible, simple to manage)
Member list with optional functional role labels — flat, no hierarchy
Active and completed challenge list
Circle-level Progress and Impact views (drawn from the Impact Record)
A simple internal communication channel (basic messaging or a linked external tool)
Explicitly out of scope
Complex governance features
Multi-step founding flows
Advanced coordination or task-management tools
Voting or decision-making mechanisms
▌
A Circle is three things: a public profile, a roster of members, and a cumulative record of completed, validated actions. Keep this definition firm during build.
3.4 Challenges - The Heart of the Pilot
The Challenge library is the mechanism through which environmental and social action is structured, reported, and recognised. It is the most feature-rich must-have module and the one most directly tied to the pilot's learning questions.
Each Challenge entry contains
Description of the action and why it matters
Required resources, tools, and estimated time commitment
Preset validation level (L1, L2, or L3) - determines the reporting flow
Preset impact metrics: 1 - 4 metrics per challenge, each with a unit (e.g., kg composted / volunteer hours/people reached)
Impact translation table: real-world equivalents (CO2e, kWh, litres, m²) with methodology and sources - content work, not developer work
Which Circles are currently working on this challenge
Which Circles have completed it
Originating Circle (if proposed by a Circle, not the core team)
Shared reflections from Circles who have completed it
Challenge proposal flow
Circles may propose new challenges. The pilot includes a simple submission form for this purpose. The evaluation and publication process may be manual in the first instance - this is acceptable and expected.
Explicitly out of scope
Automated challenge approval - manual editorial process is fine for the pilot
AI-generated challenge suggestions
Challenge versioning or forking

3.7 Progress Views
Progress is the sum of queried Impact Record rows. Available at four scales for the pilot:
Guardian level - all validated reports by this Guardian, summed by metric and shown over time
Circle level - all validated reports by Circle members, summed the same way
City/region level - all Circle aggregates within a geographic area
Network level - the full pilot total, used in the public-facing impact dashboard
Design principle: each metric streams up independently. There is no conversion or collapsing into a single score. A Circle's progress page shows, for example: 340 kg composted / 47 trees planted / 180 volunteer hours — side by side, each honest in its own unit.
▌
Progress over time — a simple timeline view — matters more than static totals. Showing continuity and effort across weeks and months is itself a Guardian's value. The timeline view is not optional.
3.8 Contribution Profile - Recognition Without Competition
The Contribution Profile answers one question: who is this Guardian or Circle, and what difference have they made? It is a mirror and a story, not a scoreboard. It makes effort legible without asking anyone to compare themselves to others.
Layer 1 - The Trace
The list of Impact Record rows for one Guardian or Circle - the same rows that Progress sums, displayed individually and chronologically.
Challenges completed, with domain (soil / water / energy / biodiversity / community / waste)
Validation level reached for each
Estimated time committed
Months active - a continuity indicator, not a streak mechanic
Challenges where this Guardian served as a peer verifier for others
Layer 2 - Contribution Markers (8 for pilot launch)
Markers are automatically earned when the Impact Record meets certain conditions. They crystallise something real that has already happened. They are not performance rewards. Earned silently, discovered when a Guardian visits their profile.
Marker
Earned When the Record Shows…
Why It Matters
First Circle
At least one completed, validated report exists
Marks the transition from intention to action
Deep Roots
At least one Level 3 peer-verified report
Signals the depth of commitment and community trust
Range
Reports across three or more distinct challenge domains
Recognises breadth of engagement
Sustained
At least one completed report in each of six consecutive months
Continuity matters more than intensity
Witness
Appears as a peer verifier in another Circle's Level 3 report
Rewards the collaborative act of showing up for others
Originator
Proposed a challenge, now adopted and completed by at least one other Circle
Mark's contribution to the shared knowledge commons
First Impact
At least one validated report with a real-world impact metric calculated
Connects action to a measurable difference in the world
Multiplier
Circle's challenges have generated Level 3 peer activity from 3+ other Circles
Recognises leadership through inspiration, not competition
▌
Language note: call them 'markers' or 'traces' in the interface — not 'badges' or 'achievements.' Small words, real consequences for how participants relate to the system.
Layer 3 - Impact Summary
Translates the Impact Record into real-world numbers. Available at three scales: Guardian, Circle, and network. The network-level summary should be prominently visible, exportable, and accompanied by a methodology note.
Example Guardian Impact Summary: "Your contributions in 2026: an estimated 340 kg of CO2e avoided through composting, 12 trees planted with an estimated 2.4 tonnes CO2e sequestration potential over 10 years, and 180 litres of water conserved through harvesting installation. Total time committed: approximately 47 hours across 6 challenges."

4.1 Methodology Principles
Use conservative estimates consistently. When a range exists in the literature, use the lower bound.
Cite every conversion factor. The source should be stored alongside the factor and visible on request.
Label all impact numbers as estimates with stated assumptions. Never present them as precise measurements.
Some challenge types (community capacity, advocacy) do not convert meaningfully to physical units. Report them as direct headcounts or hours only.
For the pilot, seek one independent reviewer — an academic or established climate NGO — to endorse the methodology. That endorsement is worth more than precision.
▌
Impact numbers are estimates, not measurements. State this clearly wherever they are displayed. Methodology transparency is the credibility mechanism.

5.2 Multilingual Design - Built In From Day One
Multilingual support is a must-have for the pilot. It is not an add-on. Retrofitting i18n after launch is significantly more expensive and disruptive than building it correctly from the start.
Mental model: every user-facing string in the platform is a row in a translation table. Each column is a language. The platform always reads from the column matching the user's language preference. Adding a new language means adding a column and filling in translations — no code changes, no developer involvement.
Key implementation rule
Every string in the interface must be read from the translation table from the very first screen — never hardcoded directly into the interface. Hold this rule firmly from day one.
▌
Important distinction: i18n covers the platform interface — all fixed text, labels, and instructions. It does not automatically translate participant-generated content (challenge descriptions, Circle reflections). That is content work, done by local coordinators.
6. Delivery Approach & Phasing
The pilot should be delivered in three phases. The phasing reflects technical dependencies (the Impact Record must exist before any views of it can be built) and the priority order defined in Section 2.
Phase 1 - Foundation (Weeks 1–6)
Establish the data architecture and authentication layer. Nothing else can be built without this.
Supabase project setup: database schema, auth, row-level security
Impact Record schema design and validation logic
Challenge library schema (metrics, validation levels, translation factors)
i18n framework integration — translation table pattern from day one
Registration and magic link auth flow
Basic admin interface (internal use only)
Phase 2 — Core Pilot Features (Weeks 7–14)
Build all P0 must-have user-facing features.
Circle profile pages + lifecycle state management
Challenge library browsing and adoption
Report submission flow (L1, L2, L3)
Level 2 admin review queue
Level 3 peer review flow
Progress views at all four scales (Guardian / Circle / City / Network)
Guardians Narrative content module
Phase 3 — Recognition & Polish (Weeks 15–20)
Build the Contribution Profile system and prepare for public launch.
Contribution Trace (Layer 1)
Contribution Markers — automated logic for all 8 markers (Layer 2)
Impact Summary at Guardian / Circle / Network scale (Layer 3)
Network-level impact dashboard (public-facing)
Challenge proposal submission form
Performance optimisation and QA
Soft launch with the first cohort of Circles
Post-Pilot (Not in Scope)
Commons/forum integration (Discourse)
Partners directory
Level 4 partner-verified validation
Dynamic narrative feedback loop
Complex Circle governance features
Advanced coordination or task-management tools
7. Key Risks & Decisions
Risk / Decision
Description
Recommended Action
Impact Record schema finalisation
The schema must be correct before any views are built. Changes after Phase 2 are costly.
Prioritise schema design review in Week 1–2. Get sign-off before any frontend work begins.
Impact translation factors
The content team must define conversion factors before launch. This is not developer work, but it blocks the Impact Summary feature.
Begin content/research work immediately, in parallel with Phase 1.
i18n from day one
If even one string is hardcoded, the pattern is broken. Retrofitting costs significantly more than getting it right upfront.
Make i18n compliance a code review gate from the first commit.
Level 2 admin capacity
Admin review queue requires human review of evidence. Volume is unknown. The core team must be resourced for this.
Monitor queue volume in soft launch. Design for distribution over time.
Commons deferral
Q4 (cross-Circle connection) is partially addressed by deferring Commons. Progress views provide some visibility but a less rich connection.
Accept this trade-off for the pilot. Use soft launch feedback to decide whether to accelerate Commons.
Impact number framing
If impact numbers are presented as precise measurements, credibility risk is high. The platform's integrity depends on honest framing.
Ensure "estimated" labelling and methodology links are non-negotiable design requirements, not afterthoughts.

9. Appendix — What Is Not Being Built
The following features are explicitly deferred from the pilot. They are not rejected - they are deliberately sequenced to follow the pilot learning period. Recording them here prevents scope creep and protects delivery timelines.
Feature
Reason for Deferral
Commons / Forum
Valuable but not critical for the pilot's core learning questions. Recommend Discourse integration post-pilot to avoid building from scratch.
Partners directory
Low complexity, but no pilot learning question depends on it. Deferred to post-pilot.
Level 4 (partner-verified) validation
Institutional verification requires partner relationships and process design beyond the pilot scope.
Dynamic narrative feedback loop
Circle stories feeding back into the Guardians' narrative is a compelling post-pilot feature. The pilot version is a static content module.
Complex Circle governance
Voting, multi-step founding flows, and advanced coordination tools — not needed for the pilot's definition of a Circle.
Social / OAuth login
Magic link auth is sufficient for the pilot. OAuth adds complexity with limited benefit at this stage.
AI-generated challenge suggestions
The Challenge library is human-curated for the pilot. Automation can be explored once content patterns are established.
Profile photo/media uploads at registration
Lightweight registration is a deliberate design choice for the pilot. Media can be added to profiles post-launch.
